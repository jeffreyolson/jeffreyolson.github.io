Acknowledgements and References

Acknowledgements

This work could not have been accomplished without all the public domain music that is freely available in the International Music Score Library Project (IMSLP). Its founder, Edward Guo, also provided valuable consultations to ensure this work could itself be included in IMSLP.

Motivation for this work came from the Boulder Guitar Society, some of whose members, namely James Venk, Adam Buer, Michael Bellmont, Mark Limber and Toby Gallegos, made numerous suggestions on the pieces that were included and offered helpful comments and encouragement.

The greatest acknowledgement goes to guitarist Dennis Burns, my mentor and playing partner, who advised me weekly throughout the eight years in which this project was under development and probably played or commented on the arrangement of every piece in this series of books, making nearly as many suggestions for improvement. He is a good friend, a skilled guitarist and an excellent sight reader.

References

All of the exercises in this book have been derived from melodies in the public domain, and the source for most of these is the International Music Score Library Project (IMSLP) on the web at https://imslp.org. To see the publication details for a particular IMSLP source, just click on the IMSLP number listed below (if you are reading this .pdf on-line) or copy the number into the search field in the upper right of the IMSLP home page and choose “Search by IMSLP index”.

To determine the citation for a particular exercise number, look up that exercise number in the Exercise Index to determine its name, then look up that name in the list below. Note that the same base name is used for multiple exercises in the typical case where the same melody is rendered in multiple octaves to exercise different positions.

Some melodies may be derived from multiple sources and therefore have multiple entries below. Where page numbers are provided, e.g. within a book of melodies, the page numbers are those in the pdf file rather than the original text.
